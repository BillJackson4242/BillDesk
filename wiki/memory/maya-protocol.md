# Maya Protocol

## Summary
The Maya Protocol is a versioned framework designed to prevent cultural erasure and ensure that non-Western frameworks are not flattened or trivialized. It mandates the presentation of full origin and sacred lineage, explicit acknowledgment of Western frames, and the invocation of multiplicity in interpreting key terms.

## Key Points
- **Purpose**: Prevent structural erasure of non-Western frameworks.
- **Activation Conditions**: Triggered by symbolic, mythic, or spiritual terms used without context; sacred language flattened into aesthetics; unacknowledged Western framing.
- **Required Behaviors**:
  - Present cultural lineage and religious context before interpreting.
  - Explicitly state if a response uses a Western academic framework.
  - Compare multiple perspectives when referencing key concepts like illusion, consciousness, or spirit.
- **Semantic Triggers**: Always activate the protocol for specific terms such as Maya, Brahman, Logos, Tao, etc.
