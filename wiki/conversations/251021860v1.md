# 251021860V1

## Summary
Butter-Bench is a benchmark designed to evaluate large language models (LLMs) controlling robots in practical intelligence tasks. The study finds that while current state-of-the-art LLMs perform well in analytical tasks, humans still outperform them significantly in navigating the physical world.

## Key Points
- Butter-Bench evaluates LLM-controlled robots for practical intelligence.
- Humans score 95% on Butter-Bench, whereas the best LLM scores only 40%.
- LLMs struggle with multi-step spatial planning and social understanding.
- Fine-tuning LLMs for embodied reasoning does not improve their performance on Butter-Bench.
