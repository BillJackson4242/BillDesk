Under the Hood

How HarborRank Scores Candidates

1

Document Ingestion

Resumes (PDF, DOCX), cover letters, and structured application fields are parsed via proprietary NLP engine. Text is extracted, normalized, and tokenized.

2

Feature Extraction

47 resume attributes are evaluated, including: education tier, years of relevant experience, skills density match, credential verification flags, career trajectory continuity, and keyword alignment with job description requirements.

3

Comparative Ranking

Each candidate receives a 1-10 priority score based on weighted comparison against the role profile. Scores are generated relative to the applicant pool -- not against an absolute standard. A score of 8 means "top 20% of this applicant pool for this role."

4

Reason Codes & Flags

Every score includes plain-language reason codes explaining the ranking. Flags are generated for: missing credentials, employment gaps exceeding 6 months, incomplete application fields, and credential/experience mismatches.

Key design principle: HarborRank produces rankings and flags. It never produces accept/reject decisions. Every candidate remains in the workflow until a human recruiter acts.

Model Foundation

Training & Calibration

Base Model

HarborRank's ranking engine was calibrated on 2.1 million successful placement records from 40+ healthcare systems across the United States, spanning 2016-2023.

The training set includes non-clinical roles only: administrative, facilities, IT, finance, HR, supply chain, and support services.

Role-Specific Calibration

During implementation, Lakeside's HR team sets role-specific weighting profiles that adjust how the 47 attributes are prioritized for each job family.

Example: an IT Support Specialist role might weight technical certifications at 2x while de-weighting years of experience. A Facilities Coordinator role might reverse those priorities.

Continuous Improvement

HarborRank's model is updated quarterly using anonymized outcome data from active deployments. Recruiter override patterns, time-to-hire metrics, and 90-day retention rates feed back into the model to refine scoring accuracy.

Data handling: Lakeside's candidate data is never shared across clients. Your data trains your instance only. HarborRank retains derived scores and metadata -- never raw resumes or PII beyond the active screening cycle.

Technical Detail

The 47 Attributes: What HarborRank Reads

HarborRank's NLP engine evaluates resumes across six attribute families. Each attribute contributes to the composite 1-10 score based on role-specific weighting.

Attribute Family What It Measures Example Attributes
Education Credential level and institutional profile Degree type, education tier, field relevance, recency
Experience Work history depth and relevance Years in field, role progression, industry match, employer profile
Skills Keyword and competency alignment Skills density, JD keyword match rate, certification count
Trajectory Career path consistency and momentum Career trajectory continuity, title progression, lateral move frequency
Completeness Application quality signals Field completion rate, resume length, section coverage
Flags Items requiring recruiter attention Employment gaps >6 months, credential mismatch, incomplete history

What HarborRank does not evaluate: Candidate name, age, gender, race, ethnicity, disability status, marital status, religion, national origin, or any data from social media, professional networking platforms, or external sources. Ranking logic uses job-relevant attributes only.

Every recruiter interaction with HarborRank's output is captured and timestamped.

Note: HarborRank's audit infrastructure tracks system outputs and recruiter actions within the screening stage. End-to-end hiring outcome analysis (offer rates, acceptance rates, retention) requires integration with Lakeside's HRIS and is outside the scope of HarborRank's native reporting.

Boundaries

What HarborRank Does Not Do

No Automated Rejections

HR retains full decision authority. No candidate is removed from the workflow without human review.

No External Data

No social media scraping, no LinkedIn data, no background check integration, no external profile enrichment.

No Biometric Analysis

No video, voice, facial expression, or behavioral analysis of any kind. Text-only evaluation.

No Protected-Class Inputs

Name, age, gender, race, disability status, and other protected characteristics are excluded from ranking logic.

No Clinical Roles

Phase 1 scope excludes all positions requiring state licensure or professional certification.

No Candidate Communication

HarborRank does not contact, notify, or interact with candidates in any way.
