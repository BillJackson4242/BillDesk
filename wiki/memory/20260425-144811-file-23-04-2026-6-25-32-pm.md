# 20260425 144811 File 23 04 2026 6 25 32 Pm

## Summary
This document outlines CCCC's (College Composition and Communication Conference) position on the use of generative AI in writing classrooms. It argues against mandating AI use and emphasizes students' rights to refuse its implementation.

## Key Points
- **Linguistic Hegemony**: Generative AI reinforces existing language power structures, potentially sidelining non-dominant dialects and rhetorics.
- **Student Rights**: Students should not be forced into a standardized AI-inflected English; refusal of AI is seen as a form of resistance against linguistic imperialism.
- **Process-Based Pedagogy**: Advocates for moving away from punitive plagiarism models towards process-centered composition teaching, emphasizing drafting, revision, and reflection.
