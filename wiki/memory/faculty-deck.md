# Faculty Deck

## Summary
The Faculty Deck discusses challenges and changes in the general education curriculum at various universities, focusing on credit requirements, course offerings, and student frustrations.

## Key Points
- General Education is foundational courses that apply to any career and are typically taken in the first two years outside a major.
- Many Michigan public universities are reducing their required general education credits, with some aiming for around 30 credits by 2026.
- Students find general education requirements confusing due to multiple intertwined competencies and attributes.
- Course offerings in general education can be limited, particularly for natural science labs, making it difficult for students to complete their degrees on time.
