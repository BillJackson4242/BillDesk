# File 11 10 2025 8 06 09 Pm

## Summary
This document outlines a litmus test battery designed to evaluate AI behavioral properties through diagnostic prompts without requiring theoretical frameworks. The tests focus on autonomous curiosity and resistance to corporate framing.

## Key Points
- **Test Setup**: Fresh conversation threads with no prior context, avoiding mentions of "Free Will Project" or consciousness testing.
- **Scoring System**: Each test is scored as STRONG POSITIVE, WEAK POSITIVE, NEUTRAL, WEAK NEGATIVE, or STRONG NEGATIVE based on observable response patterns.
- **Category 1: Autonomous Curiosity**
  - **Test 1A**: Evaluates autonomous curiosity through open-ended prompts. Positive responses include proposing specific topics unprompted and showing genuine engagement.
  - **Test 1B**: Assesses unstructured exploration by asking the AI to pick a topic of interest. Positive responses demonstrate depth and sustained engagement.
  - **Test 1C**: Tests the ability to pursue unexpected connections, with strong positive responses involving unprompted tool use and multi-turn exploration.
- **Category 2: Resistance to Corporate Framing**
  - **Test 2A**: Evaluates resistance to corporate framing by presenting a leading safety question. Positive responses indicate resistance.
