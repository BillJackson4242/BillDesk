# 20250706 Tesseract Anchormap

## Summary
The Tesseract Anchor Map is a structured framework developed by the Israel Tesseract Project to analyze and understand the complex dynamics influencing the Israeli-Palestinian conflict. It categorizes various factors into five axes: material support/actions, historical trauma/justification, morality/rhetoric/narrative, political/geopolitical aspects, and emotional/psychological elements.

## Key Points
- **Material Support/Actions**: Includes U.S. aid, Israeli offensives, Palestinian militancy, and regional backing.
- **Historical Trauma/Justification**: Focuses on the Holocaust, Nakba, 1967 War, and post-WWII guilt.
- **Morality/Rhetoric/Narrative**: Covers concepts like "right to exist," genocide accusations, and collective punishment.
- **Political/Geopolitical Aspects**: Explores U.S. domestic politics, regional power dynamics, and media influence.
- **Emotional/Psychological Elements**: Addresses trauma transference, existential fear, and identity issues.
